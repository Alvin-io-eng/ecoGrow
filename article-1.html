The Great Barrier Reef Recovery

A close look at coral rehabilitation progress, adaptive restoration techniques and ecological victories across one of the planet’s most important marine ecosystems.

The Great Barrier Reef has long symbolized both environmental fragility and resilience. In this full-length feature, we document how targeted reef restoration, community-led monitoring and coral gardening are driving local recovery after years of mass bleaching events.

Restoration by Design

ECO GROW’s marine teams are implementing resilient coral varieties and nursery propagation systems that increase juvenile coral survival and enhance reef structural complexity. Key outcomes include rapidly increasing living coral cover and establishing new coral nurseries that can withstand higher temperature anomalies.

The program emphasizes co-management with Indigenous custodians, scientific partners and local fishers to ensure restoration activities are ecologically sound and socially equitable.

Key Metrics

  • +22% coral coverage across monitored reef cells
  • 72 new nursery structures deployed this season
  • 4 community survey teams trained for reef health monitoring

Why It Matters

Coral reef resilience is foundational for coastal food systems, carbon capture and tourism livelihoods. Protecting this ecosystem supports biodiversity across thousands of species.

Community and Science in Action

Active field teams are collecting reef health data using underwater imaging, thermal sensors and benthic surveys. Results are shared with national research networks and used to adapt restoration priorities each quarter.

Local stewardship is central to success. Volunteers and partner fishers support reef cleanups, coral propagation and fish nursery protection across the wider marine landscape.
